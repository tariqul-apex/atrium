# Atrium Guest — Hotel / Resort Guest-Facing Mobile App

**Document owner:** rdasgupta@apexdmit.com
**Last updated:** 2026-07-16
**Status:** Draft v1.0
**Full documentation index:** [Project Overview & Documentation Index](overview.md)

The companion app to **Atrium Staff**: every request, order, and booking a guest makes here lands as a task, order, or reservation in the staff app — one platform, two surfaces.

---

## Features

1. **Booking & Pre-Arrival (G1)**
   - **Overview** — A mobile booking engine plus a pre-arrival flow that gets registration, preferences, and arrival details done before the guest reaches the property.
   - **What it does** — Lets the guest explore room types through photo galleries, video and 360° virtual tours, compare rooms side-by-side, search availability, book, and pay a deposit from the phone; then, ahead of arrival, complete the digital registration card, upload documents, state arrival time and preferences, and pre-order add-ons — all syncing into the property-management system so the front desk and housekeeping can plan.
   - **Why it is necessary** — Direct mobile booking recovers commission otherwise lost to online travel agencies, and pre-arrival registration removes the form-filling that makes queues at the desk — the guest arrives already known, and the property arrives already prepared.

2. **Mobile Check-In & Check-Out (G2)**
   - **Overview** — Contactless check-in and express check-out from the guest's own device — the desk becomes optional.
   - **What it does** — Verifies identity (document scan), captures an electronic signature, takes any balance or deposit, assigns the room, and tells the guest when it is ready; at departure, presents the folio for review and settles it, emailing the receipt — with a QR-code path for guests who never installed the app.
   - **Why it is necessary** — The queue is the single most-cited arrival friction and the check-out crush wastes the guest's last hour; self-service check-in/out removes both, cuts front-desk labor per arrival, and frees staff for the guests who want a human.

3. **Digital Room Key (G3)**
   - **Overview** — The guest's phone is the room key, issued at check-in and revoked at check-out.
   - **What it does** — Issues a Bluetooth mobile key through certified door-lock integrations once the room is ready, supports wallet-based keys, lets the guest share a key with a travel companion, and opens shared doors (gym, floor access) the stay entitles them to.
   - **Why it is necessary** — Keycards get lost, demagnetized, and queued-for at the desk; a digital key completes the contactless journey — without it, mobile check-in still ends in a key line — and eliminates card stock, encoders, and re-key trips.

4. **In-Stay Service Requests (G4)**
   - **Overview** — One place for the guest to ask for anything — housekeeping items, maintenance problems, concierge help — without picking up the phone.
   - **What it does** — Lets the guest request items (towels, pillows), report an issue with a photo, schedule housekeeping or set Do-Not-Disturb, opt out of daily cleaning for a green perk, report a lost item, request transport and mobility services (airport transfer with driver tracking, shuttle times, valet car retrieval, porter and luggage help), call for emergency or medical assistance with critical-priority routing, and see live status of each request — every request auto-routing to the right staff department with a service-level timer.
   - **Why it is necessary** — Phoned requests get lost between departments and guests never know if anything is happening; structured in-app requests are trackable and accountable, so satisfaction rises while call volume at the desk falls.

5. **Food & Beverage Ordering (G5)**
   - **Overview** — Order food and drink from any outlet on property — to the room, the pool, or a table — charged to the folio.
   - **What it does** — Presents live outlet menus with photos and dietary filters, takes orders for room delivery or location delivery anywhere on property, charges to the room or takes direct payment on local rails, and shows order status — with orders landing straight in the staff point-of-sale.
   - **Why it is necessary** — Food and beverage is the biggest ancillary-revenue line, and every barrier — finding a phone, waiting on hold, language friction — is lost spend; menu-in-hand ordering raises order volume and average ticket while cutting order-taking labor and mishears.

6. **Upsells, Offers & Add-Ons (G6)**
   - **Overview** — Personalized offers — upgrades, early check-in, late check-out, packages, experiences — surfaced at the moments guests actually buy.
   - **What it does** — Presents room upgrades and add-ons during booking, pre-arrival, check-in, and mid-stay — with the upgrade room shown visually (gallery, tour) so the guest sees what the extra spend buys; targets offers by guest profile, party, and occasion; sells experiences (tours, transfers, celebration setups); and books every acceptance straight to the folio and the operational calendar.
   - **Why it is necessary** — The guest's willingness to spend peaks at arrival and in-stay, exactly where a fixed desk sells worst; automated, well-timed offers monetize those moments at near-zero marginal labor — the app pays for itself on this feature.

7. **Guest Messaging (G7)**
   - **Overview** — Two-way chat between the guest and the property, from pre-arrival through departure.
   - **What it does** — Gives the guest one thread to the property that staff answer from the Atrium Staff inbox, plus a shared group thread for traveling parties; supports automated answers to common questions, auto-translation so guest and staff each write in their own language, and reaches guests who prefer WhatsApp or SMS through the same conversation.
   - **Why it is necessary** — Guests increasingly won't call but will message; a single answered thread catches problems while they are still fixable, deflects routine questions from the desk, and reads as attentive service — silence reads as neglect.

8. **Notifications & Campaigns (G8)**
   - **Overview** — The push layer that tells the guest the right thing at the right moment.
   - **What it does** — Pushes journey notifications — booking confirmed, check-in open, room ready, order on its way, your table is ready, folio ready — plus property campaigns (tonight's live music, spa slots open) targeted by stay stage and interests, with per-guest notification preferences.
   - **Why it is necessary** — Every other feature depends on the guest knowing when to act; timely notifications make self-service actually work, and well-targeted campaigns fill perishable inventory (tonight's tables, tomorrow's spa slots) that otherwise expires unsold.

9. **Payments, Folio & Tipping (G9)**
   - **Overview** — The guest's live bill, payable any time, on international cards or local rails — plus cashless tipping.
   - **What it does** — Shows the running folio in real time with every charge itemized, lets the guest pay a deposit, an interim balance, or the final bill in-app or by payment link, supports bKash / Nagad / SSLCommerz alongside cards, splits the bill across companions and rooms (group and family stays) or emails receipts, and takes QR-code tips routed to a person or a department.
   - **Why it is necessary** — Bill surprises at check-out are the classic satisfaction killer and dispute generator; a transparent live folio prevents them, local rails serve the payment methods Bangladeshi guests actually hold, and digital tipping keeps a motivating income stream alive as cash disappears.

10. **Digital Guidebook & Property Info (G10)**
    - **Overview** — The property's answers to every common question, in the guest's pocket.
    - **What it does** — Provides one-touch Wi-Fi connection, opening hours, amenity guides, house rules, transport and prayer-time information, and curated local recommendations, plus rich facility showcases — marketing-grade photo and video pages for the pool, spa, gym, and venues, each linking straight to its booking or ordering action — searchable, multilingual, and available offline.
    - **Why it is necessary** — "What's the Wi-Fi password?" and its hundred siblings consume front-desk hours every day; self-service answers deflect those calls at zero marginal cost and make the property feel effortless to use.

11. **Interactive Property Map (G11)**
    - **Overview** — A tap-able map of the property — every pool, restaurant, court, and venue — that links straight into booking.
    - **What it does** — Renders an interactive resort map with points of interest, live open/closed status, photo previews of each spot, and walking directions, where tapping a restaurant opens its menu (G5), tapping the spa opens booking (G12), and tapping an event shows its schedule.
    - **Why it is necessary** — On a large resort, what guests can't find they don't spend on; the map turns wayfinding into discovery and discovery into folio revenue, while cutting the "how do I get to…" traffic at the desk.

12. **Activities, Spa & Experiences Booking (G12)**
    - **Overview** — Self-service booking for everything on property that takes a reservation — activities, spa, dining, courts, kids' club, events.
    - **What it does** — Shows every activity and experience with a rich preview (photos, video, what-to-expect) and bookable slots with live capacity, lets the guest reserve and pay or charge to folio, books pool and beach amenities (cabanas, sunbeds) straight from the map, joins restaurant and facility waitlists as a virtual queue with table-ready alerts, builds a personal stay itinerary with reminders, and shows the resort's event and entertainment schedule — with every booking landing in the staff-side activity and event calendars.
    - **Why it is necessary** — Resort ancillaries are high-margin but capacity-bound and perishable; self-service booking fills slots that phone-only booking leaves empty, and a full itinerary measurably lifts guest satisfaction and length-of-stay value.

13. **Loyalty & Guest Profile (G13)**
    - **Overview** — The guest's identity across stays — preferences, history, points, and perks.
    - **What it does** — Maintains the guest's profile and preferences (room, pillow, dietary), earns and shows loyalty points with tier progress, redeems rewards (upgrades, dining, late check-out) in-app, manages the traveling group — companion app access, shared family profiles, a shared stay itinerary, and organizer spend controls — and personalizes offers and content from stay history.
    - **Why it is necessary** — Repeat direct guests are the most profitable revenue a property has; a loyalty program with visible progress and easy redemption gives guests a reason to book direct next time, and the profile is what makes personalization — everywhere else in the app — possible.

14. **Feedback & Service Recovery (G14)**
    - **Overview** — Catch the guest's verdict while they are still on property — and show them it was acted on.
    - **What it does** — Prompts short in-stay pulse ratings at smart moments, offers an always-available "something wrong?" channel, routes negative signals instantly to the duty manager's service-recovery workflow, shows the guest their issue's status, and invites satisfied guests to public reviews after checkout.
    - **Why it is necessary** — A problem surfaced in-stay is a save; the same problem surfaced on a review site is permanent public damage — in-app feedback converts detractors before checkout and channels promoters into the review scores that drive future bookings.

15. **App-less Access — QR & Web Links (G15)**
    - **Overview** — Every guest-facing flow reachable through a web link or QR code, no app install required — a cross-cutting enabler, not a screen of its own.
    - **What it does** — Serves check-in, menus and ordering, requests, chat, folio and payment, feedback, and tipping as link-and-QR web flows (from the booking email, a bedside card, a table tent), with the native app as the upgrade path for resort stays and loyalty members.
    - **Why it is necessary** — Most guests will not install an app for a two-night stay; without an app-less path the features above reach a fraction of guests — QR and link access takes adoption from the few to effectively every guest on property.

## Guest journey stages

The guest app has no staff-style roles; what a guest sees is driven by **journey stage** — where they are in the stay lifecycle — plus their stay type.

| Stage | When | What the app is for |
|-------|------|---------------------|
| **Discover & Book** | Before there is a reservation | Browse, book direct, pay deposit |
| **Pre-Arrival** | Booking confirmed → arrival day | Register, state preferences, pre-order, pre-book activities |
| **Arrival** | Arrival day | Check in, get room-ready alert, receive digital key |
| **In-Stay** | Checked in → departure day | Order, request, book, chat, tip, track the folio |
| **Departure** | Departure day | Review folio, express check-out, arrange transport |
| **Post-Stay** | After check-out | Receipt, review invitation, loyalty balance, rebook |

**Guest personas:** Leisure traveler · Business traveler · Resort family / group organizer · Event & banquet guest · Day visitor / walk-in local (F&B and day-use only) · Returning loyalty member.
