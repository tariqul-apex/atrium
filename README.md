# Atrium Staff — Hotel / Resort Operations Mobile App

---

## Features

1. **Front Desk**
   - The mobile version of the reception desk — arrivals, check-in and check-out, folio, payment, and room keys, done from anywhere on property instead of only behind the front counter.
   - Pulls up the arrival list and guest profile, checks the guest in, assigns and encodes the room key, opens and views the folio, takes payment (including local rails), and checks the guest out — in the lobby, at the car, or at an event rather than in a queue.
   - Arrival is the first impression and the best upsell moment (room upgrade, late checkout); tying it to a fixed desk creates queues, wastes staff time, and misses revenue, so mobile check-in lifts revenue at the touchpoint and lowers labor cost per arrival.

2. **Reservations**
   - A mobile reservations calendar to view, create, and edit bookings without a desktop terminal.
   - Shows the booking calendar and room availability, lets staff create and modify reservations, and syncs two-way with the property-management system so room status and inventory stay accurate.
   - No-shows and unmanaged availability directly cost occupancy revenue; live control of the calendar from the floor means bookings are captured and corrected in the moment, recovering no-shows and protecting occupancy.

3. **Housekeeping**
   - A live room-status board with cleaning checklists and minibar logging for room attendants and their managers.
   - Shows every room's state (dirty, cleaning, clean, inspected, out of order), lets attendants update status from the room, walk a step-by-step cleaning checklist with photo evidence, log minibar consumption, and raise maintenance issues — syncing both ways with the property-management system.
   - Housekeeping is the largest frontline labor pool and the gate on room readiness; paper worksheets and radio calls slow room turns and hide where each room stands, while real-time boards speed turns and get sellable rooms to the front desk faster.

4. **Maintenance (reactive)**
   - Digital work orders for engineering to receive, act on, and close repair jobs.
   - Captures a work order (often raised from another department), assigns it to a technician, and lets the technician log time, parts, and before-and-after photos, then close it — returning the room to service.
   - A broken room is an unsellable room and an unhappy guest; verbal and paper work orders get lost and drag out downtime, while tracked work orders cut room downtime and give a clear record of what was fixed.

5. **Task Management and Communication**
   - A shared task list plus messaging that replaces radios, WhatsApp, and printed sheets.
   - Assigns and tracks tasks with priority and service-level timers, routes guest requests to the right department automatically, supports one-to-one and group chat by department or shift, and lets managers broadcast announcements with read receipts.
   - Fragmented communication is where guest requests get dropped between departments and satisfaction suffers; one coordination layer lowers the cost of coordinating work and makes every task accountable to a person and a deadline.

6. **Notifications and Alerts**
   - A global push-notification layer that surfaces the thing each person needs to act on now.
   - Pushes new assignments, escalations, service-level warnings, and broadcasts; respects per-user quiet hours; and lets critical alerts (emergency, VIP arrival) bypass Do Not Disturb.
   - A task system only works if people act on time; without reliable alerts, service-level breaches and escalations go unnoticed, so notifications make the timeliness of every other feature real.

7. **Food and Beverage Point of Sale**
   - A mobile point of sale for restaurants, bars, and room service across multiple outlets.
   - Takes food and beverage orders tableside or in-room, charges to the guest room folio, and takes direct payment through local rails — across every outlet on property.
   - Food and beverage is the main ancillary-revenue engine at a resort; a mobile point of sale captures more of that spend at the point of service, speeds table turns, and posts charges cleanly to the folio instead of losing them.

8. **Staff Self-Attendance (In and Out)**
   - Staff clock themselves in and out on their own device, with break tracking.
   - Lets each staff member start and end their shift and record breaks from the app, generating an accurate personal timesheet without a supervisor or a physical punch clock.
   - Manual registers and shared punch clocks invite buddy-punching and error and cost supervisor time; self-attendance produces trustworthy hours automatically and is the raw input for labor cost.

9. **Labor Management**
   - Rostering, attendance, and timesheets — the layer that turns hours worked into managed labor cost.
   - Builds and publishes rosters, handles shift-swap requests with manager approval, supports geofenced clock-in and out, suggests staffing from the occupancy and arrivals forecast, flags overtime and coverage gaps, and auto-generates timesheets that can export to payroll.
   - Labor is the largest controllable cost line in a hotel; manual rotas over- or under-staff shifts and let overtime creep, while demand-based rostering right-sizes labor to occupancy and cuts overtime and buddy-punching.

10. **Preventive Maintenance and Assets**
    - An asset register plus scheduled, recurring maintenance that heads off failures before they happen.
    - Keeps a register of assets with location, warranty, and service history, and schedules recurring preventive tasks by time or meter reading that auto-generate work orders — sharing one work-order model with reactive maintenance.
    - Run-to-failure maintenance means expensive breakdowns, long out-of-order downtime, and shortened asset life, while planned upkeep prevents costly failures and protects the value of the property's equipment.

11. **Management Analytics and Owner Performance View**
    - A business-intelligence view for general managers and owners — the only analytics surface in the app.
    - Presents labor-cost percentage, productivity (rooms per attendant-hour), service-level health, task throughput, and occupancy and ancillary trends, with date-range and department filters and export for ownership, walled off from the operational monitoring line managers use.
    - Owners cannot cut waste they cannot see; consolidating operational data into performance figures gives ownership the numbers to act on cost and productivity, closing the loop between frontline work and business results.

12. **Inventory and Par-Stock**
    - Par-level stock tracking for minibar, linen, amenities, and food and beverage consumables.
    - Tracks par levels, lets staff count stock on the device, raises low-stock and reorder alerts, and logs consumption from the room or outlet — lighter than full procurement, which stays out of scope.
    - Stock that is not tracked walks out as shrinkage, spoils as waste, or forces expensive emergency purchasing, and uncharged minibar items are lost revenue, so par-stock control protects margin and recovers those charges.

13. **Inspections and Compliance**
    - Configurable digital inspections for health-and-safety, food safety, fire rounds, and brand standards.
    - Runs scheduled and ad-hoc inspections from templates, capturing photo evidence, scores, and pass or fail, and auto-raises corrective-action tasks when something fails.
    - Compliance carries real liability and fine risk, and paper audit binders are hard to trust and easy to lose, while digital inspections automate the evidence trail and lower liability and rework.

14. **Guest Feedback and Service Recovery**
    - In-stay feedback capture with a workflow to fix problems before the guest leaves.
    - Captures in-stay feedback (staff-assisted or by link), routes negative signals to the duty manager immediately, and tracks a complaint or glitch through a service-recovery workflow to resolution.
    - A problem caught after checkout becomes a bad public review, but caught during the stay it becomes a save — meaning fewer comps, stronger review scores, and more repeat and direct stays.

15. **Lost and Found plus Staff Safety**
    - A digital lost-and-found register combined with a lone-worker safety panic button.
    - Logs found items with photo and location, matches them to guests, and tracks return or disposal, and provides a global emergency panic button that sends an alert with location to security or the duty manager.
    - Paper lost-and-found logs are unreliable and a liability, and staff working alone need a fast way to call for help, so this ends the paper logbook and meets staff-safety compliance.

16. **Near-Field-Communication and Barcode Scanning**
    - One cross-cutting scan control reused across many features rather than a screen of its own.
    - Provides a single scan action — barcode, QR code, or near-field-communication tag — for scan-to-order at the point of sale, scan-to-count in inventory, scan-to-identify for assets and rooms, and proof-of-presence for rounds and inspections, resolving offline with a manual-entry fallback.
    - Typing item, asset, and room identifiers by hand is slow and error-prone, so scanning speeds point-of-sale and inventory capture and cuts wrong-item and lookup mistakes wherever those tasks happen.

17. **Events, Activities and Banquet**
    - Planning and day-of execution for weddings, banquets, parties, and resort activities.
    - Creates events with a run-of-show or function sheet, books space, resources, and audio-visual with conflict detection, assigns custom event-role crews each with their own checklist, offers activity slots with folio-charged sign-ups, and runs a live event-day board — with crew assignments writing back to the roster so an event within a shift adds no extra hours.
    - Events are high-margin ancillary revenue but are coordination-heavy and easy to run at a loss through chaos and over-staffing, so structured planning captures the revenue while cutting the coordination and staffing waste around it.

## Roles

| Role | Who | Core job on the app |
|------|-----|---------------------|
| **Front Desk Agent** | Reception / guest services | Arrivals, check-in and check-out, folio, payment, keys, reservations |
| **Housekeeping Attendant** | Room attendant | Clean rooms, update status, log minibar, raise issues |
| **Maintenance Technician** | Engineering / facilities | Accept and close reactive work orders with photos and parts |
| **Food and Beverage Staff** | Waiter / room service / bar | Take food and beverage orders, charge to room, take payment |
| **Line Manager** | Department supervisor (Housekeeping / Front Office / Engineering / Food and Beverage) | Monitor department, assign and reassign, build rosters, manage inventory and inspections, approve exceptions |
| **Duty Manager / General Manager** | Property manager | Cross-department overview, escalations, full access, owner performance view |
| **Owner / Above-property** | Ownership / regional | View performance figures only — no operational tasks |

**Custom event-role overlay** (event-scoped, not a base role): Event Captain · Setup Lead · Bar Lead · Audio-Visual · Registration and Host · Activity Guide · Safety Marshal · Runner.

**Notes.**
- **Line Manager is department-scoped.** A "Full (Front Office / Housekeeping / Engineering / Food and Beverage)" cell means full access only for that department's manager, hidden or view-only for others.
- **Owner Performance View is general-manager-and-owner only** — no frontline or line-manager role ever sees the business-analytics surface; line managers get live operational monitoring, not analytics.
- **Staff Safety panic button is universal to send**; only managers, the general manager, and security *receive* alerts.
